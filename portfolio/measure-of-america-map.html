Measure of America / Map, Infographic

Project: Measure of America map to promote the American Human Development Report
Date: 2009
What I did: writing, design

While I was working at the Social Science Research Council, I was tasked with creating a promotional flyer to promote and the first volume of The Measure of America. The report introduces the American Human Development Index, and measures well-being on the basis of three criteria: health (longevity), education (access to knowledge), and income (standard of living), and disaggregates the data by geography, race, and gender.

As a Geography major and a social scientist, I was thrilled to be able to put my cartography skills to use. Cartography is a powerful rhetorical tool, and I sought to use the map to make people consider the question: How does where you're born impact the way that you live and die?
