Zhongzhi: Nonprofit - Volunteer Matching Platform / Startup Weekend App

Project: Zhongzhi, entry in Startup Weekend Beijing 2014
Date: 2014
What I did: UX design, mobile UI design

I had never participated in a Startup Weekend, and decided to try my first Startup Weekend at Startup Weekend Beijing, despite not being fluent in Mandarin by any stretch of the imagination (pro tip: when words fail, there's always doodling).

My team created an app, Zhongzhi, that connects people with good causes. And we came in 2nd place.

Research & Discovery

Who are our stakeholders?

We identified two kinds of stakeholders: 1) volunteers and 2) NGOs. Then we hit the road to interview potential users.

Stakeholder #1: Volunteers

After conducting our interviews, we concluded that the biggest barrier that stopped people from volunteering was lack of time and lack of information about available opportunities.

Stakeholder #2: NGOs

The biggest pain point for NGOs was that they couldn't find people with the right skills at the right time.

Our Solution

We decided to create a mobile app that connects volunteers with nonprofit gigs.

Why mobile? We wanted to maximize convenience for both volunteers and NGOs, and everyone in China is glued to their phones anyway.

Designing for a Chinese audience was an interesting challenge as Chinese users are accustomed to UIs that are crammed with information, with very small text and icons. I spent a lot of time poring through apps like Taobao, WeChat, AliPay, so that I could make screens that felt intuitive for our target audience to use.

These are the screens that we came up with.


Home

If you open Chinese apps on your phone, this is pretty typical of what you'd see.

The circular icons allow volunteers to filter the kinds of activities they want to engage in.

The rectangles are sorting filters - users can sort by duration of project, or by distance from where the user is.

Activity Detail

We wanted to show how easy and convenient it is for people to volunteer, so we showed the following data:

  • Date of activity
  • Duration of activity (i.e. 1 hr of translation work, 1 day of volunteering at a children's home)
  • Location of activity
  • Distance from user's current location

The green call to action button at the bottom opens up a Contact form that allows people to indicate their interest in the volunteer gig.

Apply

Volunteers apply to participate in the gig by sending a message directly to the non-profit that placed the job in the database.

Nonprofit Organization Profile

Volunteers can read more about the nonprofits and see the profiles of the people who work there. We wanted to give the organizations a human face.

Volunteer Profile

Each volunteer has their own profile page, which they can fill out with a little profile blurb and which shows their location.

It also displays a feed of activities that the volunteer has helped out with.
